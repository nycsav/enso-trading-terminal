What NOT to Overestimate
AI Alone ≠ Edge
AI amplifies both good and bad vol models equally. The quant foundation must come first.
Direction Prediction
Retail-style 1-leg directional bets have the lowest institutional confidence of any options approach.
Skipping Phases
Jumping to dispersion or cross-asset fusion without a working vol surface monitor is premature optimization.

Where AI adds durable, compound value to the Enso stack — as context engines and signal fusion layers, not standalone trade pickers.

Data Requirements by Phase
APIs, feeds, and datasets needed to power each build phase. Sequenced by implementation priority.
Phase 0–1 · Core Vol Data
  • Options chain snapshot (bid/ask, IV, OI, volume) — Polygon.io, CBOE, OPRA
  • Historical volatility: 10d, 20d, 30d rolling window
  • ATM implied vol per tenor: 1W, 1M, 3M
  • Skew slope: 25Δ put IV minus 25Δ call IV
  • Earnings calendar with expected move — Bloomberg, Estimize
  • VIX spot + VIX futures term structure (VX1–VX4)
Phase 2 · Dealer Flow & GEX
  • Gamma exposure (GEX) by strike — SpotGamma, Market Chameleon, SqueezeMetrics
  • 0DTE flow: volume, OI, P/C ratio intraday
  • Dealer positioning proxy: dark pool + block trade data
  • Key gamma levels: max pain, dealer flip strike
  • Net dealer gamma sign: positive vs negative regime flag
Phase 3 · AI Signal Inputs
  • News wire (Reuters, Bloomberg first word) — Benzinga, NewsAPI
  • Analyst revision feed: EPS, price target changes
  • Fed / FOMC language structured extraction
  • Macro data release calendar: NFP, CPI, PCE, ISM
  • Unusual options activity alerts — Unusual Whales, Cheddar Flow
Phase 4 · Advanced Surface
  • Full vol surface (all strikes × tenors) — iVolatility, CBOE Datashop
  • Index vs single-name IV for dispersion: SPX vs SPX members
  • Implied correlation index: ICJ, KCJ
  • Cross-asset: CDX credit spreads, MOVE index (rate vol)
  • Transaction cost model: spread + slippage + delta hedge cost
